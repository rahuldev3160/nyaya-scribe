Focus on Poverty, Unemployment, and Human Development in India. This is IES General Economics revision for GE-04 (Paper IV) at DSE/JNU masters level. Target length: 15 minutes.

Cover these specific sub-concepts: poverty measurement methodologies — headcount ratio (H), poverty gap index (PG = average shortfall as fraction of line), squared poverty gap (FGT2 = captures inequality among the poor); Foster-Greer-Thorbecke (FGT) family of indices; official poverty lines in India — Tendulkar Committee (consumption-based, state-specific, 2011-12 = Rs 33/day urban), Rangarajan Committee (higher, calorie-plus-non-food), SECC-based deprivation — why these differ and which is more comprehensive; multidimensional poverty — Alkire-Foster method: three dimensions (health, education, living standards), 10 indicators, dual cutoff methodology; UNDP Global MPI — India's performance, trends since 2015; nutrition-poverty link — stunting and wasting data, POSHAN Abhiyaan; employment measurement — usual principal status (UPS), usual subsidiary status (USS), current weekly status (CWS), current daily status (CDS) — what each captures and why CDS gives highest unemployment; types of unemployment in India — open (measured), disguised (agricultural), structural (skill mismatch), frictional; NSSO/PLFS data trends — labour force participation rate (especially female LFPR), unemployment rate by gender and education; skill development ecosystem — PMKVY outcomes and criticism, National Apprenticeship Promotion Scheme; human development in India — NHM: ASHA, Janani Suraksha Yojana, IMR and MMR trends; Ayushman Bharat PM-JAY — coverage, limitations; RTE Act and learning outcomes crisis (ASER data); NEP 2020 — foundational literacy, 5+3+3+4 structure, vocationalisation; India's HDI rank and its components — income vs health vs education scores.

DO NOT cover: agricultural policy (MSP, MGNREGA, APMC) — those are in Audio 1a; labour laws and labour codes (Audio 1c); urbanisation and Harris-Todaro model (Audio 1c).

For every measurement concept: explain why it matters for policy. "The squared poverty gap matters because headcount ratio is blind to how poor the poor are. If you give a small transfer to the least poor among the poor, they cross the line and headcount falls — but the very poorest are untouched. FGT2 squares the poverty gap, so it rises sharply with depth and inequality among the poor — it targets the worst-off."

Structure: Open with the 2025 question on multidimensional poverty versus income poverty and the 2024 question on disguised unemployment. Build from measurement through employment concepts to human development programmes. End with 3 most likely IES 2026 patterns: (1) compare Tendulkar vs Rangarajan vs Alkire-Foster — methodology and policy implications, (2) LFPR trends in India — female participation paradox and its explanations, (3) Ayushman Bharat design and coverage gaps.
