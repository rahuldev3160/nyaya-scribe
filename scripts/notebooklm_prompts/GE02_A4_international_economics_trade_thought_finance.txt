Focus on three linked topics: International Economics (trade theory), Economic Thought (history of ideas), and Financial and Capital Markets. This is IES General Economics revision for GE-02 (Paper II) at DSE/JNU masters level.

INTERNATIONAL ECONOMICS — cover: Ricardo's comparative advantage — opportunity cost formulation, numerical examples; Haberler's opportunity cost theory; Heckscher-Ohlin theorem — factor endowments, factor price equalisation (Samuelson), Stolper-Samuelson theorem; Rybczynski theorem; gains from trade — static and dynamic; terms of trade — commodity, income, factorial terms; trade policy instruments — tariffs (specific, ad valorem), quotas, subsidies, voluntary export restraints; theory of tariffs — optimum tariff, effective rate of protection; regional trade arrangements — free trade area, customs union, common market; trade creation and trade diversion (Viner); new trade theory — economies of scale, imperfect competition, intra-industry trade.

ECONOMIC THOUGHT — cover: Mercantilism — bullionism, balance of trade doctrine, state intervention; Physiocrats — Quesnay's Tableau Economique as precursor to circular flow; Classical school — Adam Smith's invisible hand and division of labour, Ricardo's labour theory of value and rent, Malthus; JS Mill as bridge; Marx — dialectical materialism, surplus value, tendency of rate of profit to fall; Marginalist revolution — Jevons, Menger, Walras; Keynesian revolution — historical context of the Great Depression, General Theory; Monetarist school — Friedman; rational expectations and New Classical school; institutionalist school.

FINANCIAL AND CAPITAL MARKETS — cover: structure of financial markets — money market versus capital market; primary versus secondary markets; stock market efficiency — weak, semi-strong, strong form (EMH); equity market functioning; gilt market and government securities; banking sector role in financial intermediation; derivatives markets — futures, options, hedging versus speculation; Asian financial crisis 1997, Global Financial Crisis 2008, Eurozone crisis — causes, transmission, and lessons; finance and economic development link.
