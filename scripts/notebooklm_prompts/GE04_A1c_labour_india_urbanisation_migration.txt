Focus on Labour Markets and Urbanisation in India. This is IES General Economics revision for GE-04 (Paper IV) at DSE/JNU masters level. Target length: 15 minutes.

Cover these specific sub-concepts: LABOUR MARKETS IN INDIA — formal vs informal sector segmentation — definition of informal employment (no social security, no written contract), share of informal employment in total employment (over 90 percent); dual labour market — primary (formal, high wage, stable) vs secondary (informal, low wage, casual); wage determination in informal sector — competitive, efficiency wage, or bargained?; gig economy — platform workers (Ola, Swiggy) — regulatory grey zone; labour law framework in India — four Labour Codes: Wage Code 2019 (universal minimum wage, floor wage), Industrial Relations Code 2020 (raised threshold for closures and layoffs from 100 to 300 workers), Social Security Code 2020 (extending ESIC and EPFO to gig workers, platform workers), Occupational Safety Code 2020 — consolidation rationale and criticisms (flexibility vs protection trade-off); minimum wage — setting mechanism (tripartite boards, RSBY, MGNREGA as de facto floor), enforcement gaps, OECD comparison; child labour — definition under Child Labour Act 1986 and 2016 amendment, hazardous vs non-hazardous distinction, ground reality; trade union movement in India — major federations, declining density in formal sector, no presence in informal sector; ILO core labour standards — four clusters: freedom of association, elimination of forced labour, abolition of child labour, non-discrimination; India's ratification gaps.

URBANISATION AND MIGRATION — urbanisation rate (35 percent in 2011, projected 50 percent by 2050); census definition of urban (population, density, non-agricultural occupation criteria); urbanisation drivers — pull (wage gap, services) vs push (agricultural distress, climate shocks); Harris-Todaro model — rural-urban migration continues as long as urban expected wage exceeds rural wage; expected urban wage = formal wage times probability of getting formal job; equilibrium creates urban open unemployment; policy implication: creating urban jobs accelerates rural-urban migration and may not reduce unemployment; informal urban sector — ILO view (productive absorption) vs Mazumdar view (residual queue); smart cities mission — 100 smart cities, ICT infrastructure, area-based development vs whole-city; AMRUT — infrastructure for smaller cities; PMAY-Urban — credit-linked subsidy for urban housing, slum rehabilitation; migration and remittances — domestic migration scale (400 million), circular migration patterns, seasonal migrants in construction and brick kilns; migration and COVID — reverse migration 2020 as policy failure.

DO NOT cover: poverty measurement or MGNREGA (Audio 1a/1b); monetary policy or banking (Audio 2); fiscal policy or GST (Audio 3).

Structure: Open with the 2025 question on Labour Code consolidation and the 2024 question on the Harris-Todaro model. Build labour markets first (more questions), then urbanisation. End with 3 most likely IES 2026 patterns: (1) Labour Code consolidation — rationale, key changes, and labour market implications, (2) Harris-Todaro model — derive equilibrium, explain policy paradox, evaluate limits, (3) informal employment in India — define, measure, and propose social security solutions.
